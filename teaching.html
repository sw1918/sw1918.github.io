Directed Research Groups (DRG)

Analyzing and Supporting Twitter-Based Science Communication

Co-facilitated with Keri Mallari and overseen by Gary Hsieh and Katharina Reinecke.

Spring 2020.

In this DRG, the primary tasks include identifying relevant HCI researchers on Twitter, using text classification to categorize Twitter users, and designing and prototyping a system (and its underlying algorithms) to help understand the reach of science communication on Twitter. We will also be reading relevant papers about science communication on social media, NLP, and network science.


HCI, Journalism & Misinformation: Understanding how journalists process, analyze and make sense of data

Co-facilitated with Melinda McClure Haughey and overseen by Kate Starbird.

Spring 2019

Students of this DRG are guided in research activities aimed at understanding and aiding journalists' work when using social media data. These include interviewing local journalists, analyzing data, reading current literature, and designing tools to help journalists deal with the vast amount of data in today's social media landscape.
