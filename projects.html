Science Communication

I am currently working with Professors Gary Hsieh and Katharina Reinecke to better understand how scientists communicate their research findings to the public. The increasing number of social media venues like Twitter, Facebook, Reddit, Youtube, and blog platforms like Medium, have offered numerous opportunities for scientists to reach non-expert audiences. Through this project, I hope to better understand how today's researchers take advantage of these platforms, the challenges they face when communicating their research, and how those challenges might be addressed.

Science News and Social Media

Related to the above project, I am also interested in understanding how people react to and engage with science nows on social media platforms like Twitter and Reddit. I hope to learn more about how different platform features affect users' interactions with science news, such as sharing and commenting, and how those actions in turn affect what users take away from the stories.

Journalists and Social Media

I am currently working with Professor Kate Starbird to better understand how journalists use social media for news sourcing and verification. Recent work has been done in developing technological tools to help journalists collect, visualize, and verify social media data, but it remains unclear how the strategies and user needs of different types of journalists (e.g. editors vs. reporters), topics (e.g. national politics vs. local news), and organizations (e.g. print newspaper vs. online publication) vary, and to what extent. I hope to better understand the breadth of these needs, to better inform the design of tools and techniques for journalists engaged in this type of work.
