I am a 2nd year PhD Student in Human Centered Design and Engineering at the University of Washington. For more about me and my work, see my about page or check out a list of my research projects below.

Science Communication

I am currently working with Professors Gary Hsieh and Katharina Reinecke to better understand how scientists communicate their research findings to the public. The increasing number of social media venues like Twitter, Facebook, Reddit, Youtube, and blog platforms like Medium, have offered numerous opportunities for scientists to reach non-expert audiences. Through this project, I hope to better understand how today's researchers take advantage of these platforms, the challenges they face when communicating their research, and how those challenges might be addressed.

Methods

  • Survey
  • Semi-structured interviews
  • Modified grounded theory

Submitted for publication


Science News and Social Media

I am interested in understanding how people react to and engage with science nows on social media platforms like Twitter and Reddit. I hope to learn more about how different platform features affect users' interactions with science news, such as sharing and commenting, and how those actions in turn affect what users take away from the stories.

Methods

  • Experimental design
  • Semi-structured interviews
  • Bayesian analysis

Depression in Cancer Care Settings

I am working with Jina Suh, Amy Bauer, Gary Hsieh, Jesse Fann, and James Fogarty to better understand the dual journies of patients undergoing depression treatment while battling cancer. Through interviews with patients, social workers, oncologists, and administrators, we hope to collect data about the intersecting challenges facing all stakeholders within this context, which will inform the design of technology interventions to support patients and providers in improved communication and collaboration.

Methods

  • Semi-structured interviews
  • Modified grounded theory
  • User-centered design

Submitted for publication
