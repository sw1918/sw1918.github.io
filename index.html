Hi! I am a 5th year PhD candidate in Human Centered Design and Engineering (HCDE) at the University of Washington (UW). I study how researchers can better engage with the public online, and how to communicate about controversial scientific topics. For more about me and my work, see my about page or check out a list of my ongoing research projects below.

Ongoing Projects

Empowering non-experts to assess scientific research

I am currently working to understand how to build tools to help non-experts better assess bodies of scientific literature. Building on my previous work on vaccine meta-summaries, I hope to extend these public-facing summaries with expanded tools and guidance to help people can use scientific information to make more informed decisions about health, safety, education, and other important topics.

Methods

  • Web development
  • Human-centered design
  • Experiments

In progress


Providing Insights about Researchers' Audiences on Twitter

I am currently working to examine how easily researchers can use Twitter to engage with the broader public about their research. Some of the questions I'm tackling include, what is the average researcher's reach on Twitter? What conversations about research are happening on the platform? What tools can we provide to help researchers connect with the public about research that's relevant to them?

Methods

  • Social network analysis
  • Qualitative coding
  • Software development
  • Qualitative user research
